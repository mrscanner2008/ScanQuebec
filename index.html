About

The Passion

Radio scanning is more than a hobby — it's a window into the invisible infrastructure that keeps a city running. From the encrypted trunked systems of Montréal's emergency services to the raw analog chatter of rail dispatch, every frequency tells a story.

Based in Montréal, Québec, my monitoring setup covers VHF/UHF emergency services, aircraft communications (ADS-B + voice), maritime, rail, and government channels. SDR has transformed what's possible for the passionate radio monitor.

The MTQ camera network is a fascinating complement to radio monitoring — when you hear an incident on scanner, you can often pull up the nearest traffic camera to see real-world conditions. That synthesis of audio monitoring and visual confirmation is uniquely satisfying.

This site serves as a reference for fellow enthusiasts in the Québec region — frequency lists, gear reviews, and access to the MTQ camera network.
